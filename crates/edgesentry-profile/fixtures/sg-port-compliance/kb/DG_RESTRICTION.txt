Dangerous Goods — Singapore Port Entry Requirements

Vessels carrying dangerous goods as defined by the IMDG Code must obtain prior approval
from the Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore (MPA) before entering port limits.

Pre-arrival requirements:
- Submit Dangerous Goods (DG) pre-notification via Port+ at least 24 hours before arrival
- Obtain berth allocation from MPA Port Operations Control Centre (POCC)
- IMDG-compliant packing, labelling, and stowage documentation required

Classes requiring special approval:
- Class 1 (Explosives): requires MPA and Singapore Civil Defence Force approval
- Class 2.3, 6.1, 8 (Toxic, Corrosive): enhanced inspection regime
- Radioactive materials (Class 7): nuclear regulatory approval required

Enforcement:
- Non-compliant vessels subject to detention, fines, or denial of entry
- Master and shipper jointly liable for false dangerous goods declarations

Reference: IMDG Code (Amendment 41-22)
