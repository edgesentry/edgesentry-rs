Site Safety Procedure §4.1 — Exclusion Zone Access Control

Designated exclusion zones are areas where vehicle entry is prohibited without explicit
authorisation from the site safety coordinator. Zones are marked by physical barriers and
floor markings and are active whenever lifting, overhead work, or hazardous operations
are in progress.

Any powered industrial truck detected inside an exclusion zone must:
  (a) stop immediately,
  (b) reverse out of the zone by the shortest safe path,
  (c) not re-enter until the zone is cleared and the operator has received written
      authorisation.

An automated EXCLUSION_ZONE_BREACH event triggers an immediate alert to the site safety
officer and is recorded as a critical incident requiring root-cause analysis within 24 hours.

Reference: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178(n)(4) — Powered Industrial Truck Operating Safety.
